ICD-10 uses the terminology “amphetamines” to refer to the drug class that includes “methamphetamine”, as well as MDMA (ecstasy) and other pharmaceutical stimulants but excludes cocaine. Amphetamine-induced deaths refer to deaths where amphetamines (as defined by ICD-10 terminology) were considered to be the underlying cause of death. Causes of death data undergo a revision process. Data for 2018 and 2019 are preliminary (indicated by 'x' in most of the plots) and subject to another two rounds of revision. Data for 2017 are revised (indicated by 'o' in most of the plots) and subject to another round of revision. Data for 2016 and earlier years are final. Data with number of deaths less than or equal to 5 have been removed and this can be visible as gaps in the data series.

Plots also showing "All drug-induced deaths" display data on drug-induced deaths involving amphetamines. For your information, these data on drug-induced deaths involving amphetamines are also shown under the plots for "Drug-induced deaths by drug and age".

NOTE: Data by sex and for the 65 to 74 years and 75 to 84 years age groups are unavailable due to small numbers.
