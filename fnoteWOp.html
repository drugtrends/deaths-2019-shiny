These figures shows opioid-induced deaths with other drugs (e.g. benzodiazepines, alcohol) contributing to the death. Opioid-induced deaths are defined as deaths where opioid toxicity was considered to be the underlying cause. Causes of death data undergo a revision process. Data for 2018 and 2019 are preliminary (indicated by 'x' in most of the plots) and subject to another two rounds of revision. Data for 2017 are revised (indicated by 'o' in most of the plots) and subject to another round of revision. Data for 2016 and earlier years are final. Data with number of deaths less than or equal to 5 have been removed and this can be visible as gaps in the data series.

Plots also showing "All drug-induced deaths" display data on drug-induced deaths involving the corresponding drug. For your information, these data on drug-induced deaths are also shown under the plots for "Drug-induced deaths by drug, jurisdiction and sex" or "Drug-induced deaths by drug and age".

NOTE: Data for opioid with antiepileptic & sedative-hypnotic drugs, unspecifiedare unavailable for the years before 2015 due to small numbers.
